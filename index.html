About Me

    Hello! My name is Jonathan Poisson and I am a Junior at the University of Florida. My story is pretty different from typical computer science students, especially since I am not a computer science major! I am glad you found this website and hopefully I can explain to you what goals I plan on achieving and how I will make them happen.

    The obvious question is why music? Well, when I first arrived at UF I was not sure what exactly I wanted to do and was subsequently what I truely loved doing. Having played music since the 4th grade, it was an easy choice to pursue music. UF, however, offers a program where music students can get a bachelor in music while taking required classes for an "outside field." This path was perfect for me, I did not want to let go of music but I know that a career in music perforamnce is risky and difficult to achieve. After a brief encounter with pre-med as my outside field (everyone seems to do pre-med) I decided on psychology. My hope was to obtain my bachelor degree in music and then take my knowledge of psychology with me to law school. I followed this track for almost two years, but something still did not feel right, I did not love what I was learning on the psychology side of my courses and I did not look forward to the future.

    Computer science to the rescue! I have always treated programming as a hobby, beginning my learning in highschool building calculator programs for fellow students to solve the quadratic formula. While zoning out in my abnormal psychology class I came across the language of python. This language was easy to pick up and my love for programming and computer science was reborn. I begain watching every tutorial I could find, building little scrips that would complete fun, basic tasks such as opening a random youtube music video every two hours for a nice laugh. Fall of 2014 was when I decied to make the switch that I knew was the right one for me.

    Fast forward to this semeseter, Fall 2016. Currently I still have my Music Major and my outside field consists of a minor in computer science. I love what I am learning and I look forward to all of my current and future classes. My goal is to take my minor and pursue a masters degree in computer science. I know I am behind, but my determination and true enjoyment of what I learning makes up for my lack of a major in the field.I am always reading ahead in my computer science classes, building web applications and gaining experience in field by taking on various web projects while working with the UF Cancer Research Center on more software oriented projects. I plan to obtain a career in either full-stack web development or software development . I have never felt this much drive and determination to create great things and put my creativity to work. Combining my passions with my education is a great feeling and I know that I will not fail.

Mubra.org


Mubra.org is a project I designed to help the University of Florida Music Building keep track of Students' recital attendance. The current system uses a paper system that is slow and unreliable so using PHP I created a database driven system to keep track of recital attendance for students by securely logging them into and out of recitals when they attend. The website was built entirely in OOP PHP as I wanted a chance to learn how each step of the process works (essentially inventing the wheel for each step) but now I am in the process of rebuilding the application using Python and the Django framework. This application will be used for the first time in the Fall of 2015.

Pong


Pong on Git

Instructions

  • Java must be running in browser and this website should be added to your Java exceptions through the Java configuration program
  • Press 'ENTER' to restart game
  • Hit the ball past the computer opponent 5 times to win
  • Pressing 'ESCAPE' ends the game until the page is refreshed

Pong was another "retro" game I made in java. When I first finished the game is was impossible to win because the compute controlled paddle would track the movements of the ball perfectly. This means the player could never score. I was later able to develop an algorithim that allows the computer to move freely on its own outside of the ball's movement, therefore allowing it to miss the ball. This game was my first experience with computer AI and really made me think about everything the computer must do to allow the player to actually have a chance at winning
